Assignment 4


Preset 1: Pointillism 
-------------------------------
Preset 1 was just the result of me messing with the variables, when I found something that started turning all the agents into single dots. After that I kept experimenting, resulting in Pointillism. Essentially it freezes any current design in place, shattering it into clusters of individual agents. The best part is that, while it looks static, looking closer you can still see microcosms of agents shifting, clustering into larger trails, which gives the whole simulation this weird shaky feeling.

Preset 2: Madness
-------------------------------
I've been into reading a lot of cosmic horror recently, and Madness is a reflection of that. The dark red helps keep it more hidden in the dark, and the slow languid trails of agents help add a somewhat fascinating display. My favorite part of this preset is how the simulation moves along just slow enough that you're never quite sure how much stuff is changing until you look away and look back and see the completely different shapes.

Preset 3: Spiderweb
-------------------------------
Spiderweb is a simpler one, I had been playing around with large sensor distances and low decay and i noticed a lot of paths beginning to form and linger. Applying this to a larger field resulted in these beautiful long crisscrossing strands forming before slowly condensing into a fewer ones that were more interesting, sweeping and branching, trading agents between them, constantly shifting and moving between these solid paths that themselves were ever so slowly shifting.

Preset 4: Flesh
-------------------------------
This preset stems again from the cosmic horror interest, combined with my natural desire to try and make things as chaotic as possible. By increasing transparency, allowing more rotation, and making each agent larger, the result is a screen filled with agents, only small gaps of black poking through. Making it tan just realized its full potential: if you look at the piece as a whole, let your eyes slide out of focus, it almost looks like the pulsing, writhing flesh of some massive creature.
