Assignment 1

My aesthetic intent for this project was to try and capture something that was interesting to watch. While it continually repeats, it is still visually pleasing and, after some happy accidents during creations, has come to reflect myself in ways I did not expect. The coloration of pink, yellow, and blue in those hues are reminiscent of the pansexual flag, representing an aspect of myself that, like the animation, is constantly in flow, shifting with uncertainty. The more staticky elements reflecting an underlying chaos that I like to believe I have, as personally life without a little chaos feels a touch dull.

The technical intent was more to experiment than anything. I'm not as familiar with shaders as some, so I moreso set out to toy around with the language, creating something interesting. I did want to see how different mathematic functions could be used to create interesting effects, and to this goal I certainly succeeded, as nine of the functions are math-related.

I showed my animation to a classmate, who really enjoyed the colors. He also thought the combination of bright colors with soft gradient edges gave it an old painted look, and the swirling parts in the middle reminded him of ribbons, which gave a cool sense of deeper space beyond. His only point of critique was that when all the lines dive towards the bottom, it feels a bit sudden. Overall, I think that most of the feedback matched with my artistic intentions, and I'm relatively happy with this piece.
